Who I Am

Ijagbemi David

I am a first-year Cybersecurity student at MIVA Open University, genuinely passionate about securing the digital world. Raised in Kwara, I saw firsthand how technology from fintech solutions to e-commerce platforms were transforming lives, and I became determined to protect these innovations from cyber threats and ensure safer digital experiences for everyone.

My academic journey began with curiosity and has grown into a structured passion. I spend my weekends working on side projects, exploring open-source security tools, participating in Capture The Flag challenges, and occasionally mentoring younger students in my community on online safety and basic cybersecurity practices.

I am particularly fascinated by ethical hacking and vulnerability assessment. Through self-directed learning and online courses I have gained hands-on experience with tools such as Wireshark Metasploit and Burp Suite. These experiences have strengthened my ability to think like an attacker while building defensive strategies that anticipate real-world threats.

Looking ahead I aim to pursue internships and certifications such as CompTIA Security+ and Certified Ethical Hacker to deepen my expertise. Ultimately I aspire to build a career that combines technical proficiency with a commitment to making cybersecurity accessible and effective for communities across Africa. I am excited to continue learning collaborating with like-minded professionals and turning my passion into meaningful impact in the digital security space.
